Call for Papers

Proactive and personalized agents are quickly moving from research prototypes to everyday interfaces for search, dialogue, and recommendation. As these systems become more capable, they increasingly shape what users notice, learn, and decide, often by taking initiative: suggesting next steps, surfacing information users did not explicitly request, and adapting responses to a user’s goals, context, and history. This shift raises new opportunities for “information fostering,” sensemaking, and learning, but also introduces risks around agency, consent, trust, overreach, and unwanted personalization.

Despite rapid progress, conversations about proactive and personalized agents remain fragmented across communities: interactive information retrieval, recommender systems, HCI, dialogue, responsible AI, and cognitive/behavioral science. At the same time, translating new ideas into real-world systems often involves practical considerations, such as latency, privacy, evaluation in the wild, user control, safety policies, and organizational accountability, that deserve deeper shared attention. Bridging these gaps is now urgent: we need shared conceptualizations of proactivity, principled design patterns for mixed-initiative interaction, and evaluation frameworks that reflect what users actually experience.

With this motivation, we are organizing the Workshop on Human-Centered Proactive and Personalized Agents for Interactive Information Access at CHIIR’26. The workshop aims to (1) bring together researchers and practitioners across IR, HCI, RecSys, NLP, cognitive/behavioral science and Responsible AI; (2) develop a shared agenda and design/evaluation paradigms for proactive agents; and (3) surface open research questions that can guide the next generation of human-centered agentic systems.

We invite 1-page extended abstracts (text-only; ~500 words), including position/perspective papers, empirical studies, surveys, and early-stage ideas. Submissions will be peer reviewed for relevance, clarity, and their potential to spark discussion. Accepted contributions will be presented at the workshop, and at least one author must attend in person to present.

Scope

Submissions should broadly relate to proactive and/or personalized agents for interactive information access. We welcome work across design, systems, interaction, evaluation, and responsible deployment.

Topics of interest include (but are not limited to):

  • Human-centered definitions and boundaries of proactivity (initiative, timing, appropriateness)
  • Personalization and user modeling for agentic systems
  • Mixed-initiative interaction patterns and proactive interventions
  • Proactive agents for search, recommendation, and exploratory discovery
  • Evaluation ideas (lightweight or rigorous): metrics, user studies, benchmarks, case studies
  • Trust, consent, privacy, safety, and governance in proactive systems
  • Failure modes (overreach, manipulation, interruptions) and mitigation strategies
  • Domain applications (education, health, productivity, community settings, etc.)
