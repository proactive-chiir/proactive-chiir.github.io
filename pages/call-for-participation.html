Call for Papers

We invite submissions to the ACM CHIIR 2026 1st Workshop on Human-Centered Proactive and Personalized Agents for Interactive Information Access. This is the first edition of the workshop, and we are intentionally keeping submissions low-friction and collaborative to encourage early ideas, community discussion, and cross-disciplinary exchange.

Submission format (simple by design)

  • What to submit: a ~500-word extended abstract (plain text)
  • Where: submit via the Google Form linked below
  • Types: position / perspective / empirical snapshots / brief surveys or syntheses
  • Goal: spark discussion—early-stage work and open problems are welcome

Important dates

  • Submission deadline: January 15, 2026 (11:59 PM AoE)
  • Notification: TBA
  • Workshop at CHIIR 2026: TBA

Attendance & presentation requirement

To keep the workshop highly interactive, at least one author of each accepted submission must register, attend, and present at the workshop. Presentation format will be lightweight (e.g., short talk and/or poster) and will be finalized closer to the workshop date.

Scope

Submissions should broadly relate to proactive and/or personalized agents for interactive information access. We welcome work across design, systems, interaction, evaluation, and responsible deployment.

Topics of interest include (but are not limited to):

  • Human-centered definitions and boundaries of proactivity (initiative, timing, appropriateness)
  • Personalization and user modeling for agentic systems
  • Mixed-initiative interaction patterns and proactive interventions
  • Proactive agents for search, recommendation, and exploratory discovery
  • Evaluation ideas (lightweight or rigorous): metrics, user studies, benchmarks, case studies
  • Trust, consent, privacy, safety, and governance in proactive systems
  • Failure modes (overreach, manipulation, interruptions) and mitigation strategies
  • Domain applications (education, health, productivity, community settings, etc.)
